# Lesson 2: Greetings and Introductions

## Introduction

Welcome to Lesson 2! In this lesson, we'll focus on common Spanish greetings and how to introduce yourself. These are essential phrases that you'll use in everyday conversations.

Here are some of the most common greetings you'll use in Spanish.

## Section 2: Introducing Yourself

Let's learn how to introduce yourself in Spanish.

1. **Me llamo [your name]** - "My name is [your name]"
2. **Encantado/a** - "Pleased to meet you" (Use 'Encantado' if you're male and 'Encantada' if you're female)
3. **¿Cómo estás?** - "How are you?" (Informal)
4. **Bien, gracias. ¿Y tú?** - "Well, thank you. And you?"

## Section 3: Introduction Phrases

Once you've greeted someone, you'll want to introduce yourself or ask for their name. Here are some useful phrases:

-   ¿Cómo te llamas? (What is your name?)
-   Me llamo... (My name is...)
-   Mucho gusto (Nice to meet you)
-   ¿De dónde eres? (Where are you from?)

## Section 4: Practice Dialogues

Practicing with dialogues can help you understand how to use greetings and introductions in real-life situations. Here are two dialogues for practice:

### Dialogue 1: Meeting Someone for the First Time

-   **Person A:** ¡Hola! Me llamo María. ¿Y tú, cómo te llamas?
-   **Person B:** ¡Hola, María! Me llamo Carlos. Encantado de conocerte.
-   **María:** Encantada, Carlos. ¿Cómo estás?
-   **Carlos:** Bien, gracias. ¿Y tú?
-   **María:** También bien, gracias.

### Dialogue 2: Greeting a Friend in the Morning

-   **Person A:** ¡Buenos días, José! ¿Cómo estás hoy?
-   **José:** ¡Buenos días! Estoy bien, ¿y tú?
-   **Person A:** Muy bien, gracias. ¿Listo para el día?
-   **José:** Sí, vamos a tener un buen día.

Remember to practice these dialogues aloud. You can also record yourself to improve your pronunciation and fluency. Try to understand the context of each phrase and how they are used in a conversation.

## Exercises

1. **Listening and Repeating:** Practice saying the greetings and introducing yourself.
2. **Dialogue Practice:** Act out the practice dialogues with a partner or recording yourself.
3. **Vocabulary Quiz:** Test your knowledge of the new words and phrases.

## Conclusion

Well done! You've learned how to greet people in Spanish and introduce yourself. These are foundational skills that you'll use constantly as you continue learning.

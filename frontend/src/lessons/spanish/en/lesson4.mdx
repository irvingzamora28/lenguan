# Lesson 4: Numbers and Basic Counting

## Introduction

Welcome to Lesson 4! Numbers are essential in everyday life, from telling time to shopping and beyond. This lesson will cover numbers from zero to one hundred in Spanish.

## Section 1: Numbers 0-10

Let's start with the basics. Here are the numbers from zero to ten in Spanish:

| Number | Spanish | Example Usage                    |
| ------ | ------- | -------------------------------- |
| 0      | Cero    | Tengo cero dudas.                |
| 1      | Uno     | Uno más uno es dos.              |
| 2      | Dos     | Tengo dos hermanos.              |
| 3      | Tres    | Somos tres en casa.              |
| 4      | Cuatro  | Hay cuatro estaciones.           |
| 5      | Cinco   | Me quedan cinco minutos.         |
| 6      | Seis    | Seis días a la semana.           |
| 7      | Siete   | Mi número de la suerte es siete. |
| 8      | Ocho    | Ocho rebanadas de pizza.         |
| 9      | Nueve   | Son las nueve de la mañana.      |
| 10     | Diez    | Diez alumnos en clase.           |

## Section 3: Forming Compound Numbers

Once you're comfortable with the numbers up to 30, forming higher numbers is simply a matter of combining what you've learned.

### Numbers 21-29 (Veintiuno to Veintinueve)

Numbers from 21 to 29 are formed by using the base "veinti" followed by the unit number. Unlike other compound numbers, these do not include "y" in between.

| Number | Spanish      | How to Form     | Example Usage                               |
| ------ | ------------ | --------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| 21     | Veintiuno    | veinti + uno    | Tengo veintiún años.                        |
| 22     | Veintidós    | veinti + dos    | Hay veintidós estudiantes.                  |
| 23     | Veintitrés   | veinti + tres   | Son las veintitrés horas.                   |
| 24     | Veinticuatro | veinti + cuatro | Cuesta veinticuatro euros.                  |
| 25     | Veinticinco  | veinti + cinco  | Quedan veinticinco días para Navidad.       |
| 26     | Veintiséis   | veinti + seis   | Son veintiésis alumnos en total.            |
| 27     | Veintisiete  | veinti + siete  | Vivo a veintisiete kilómetros de la ciudad. |
| 28     | Veintiocho   | veinti + ocho   | Cuesta veintiocho euros por persona.        |
| 29     | Veintinueve  | veinti + nueve  | El edificio tiene veintinueve pisos.        |

### Numbers 31 and Above

For numbers 31 and above, you generally use the tens number (treinta, cuarenta, cincuenta, etc.) followed by "y" and then the unit number.

| Tens      | +   | Unit | =     | Spanish          |
| --------- | --- | ---- | ----- | ---------------- |
| Treinta   | y   | uno  | (31)  | Treinta y uno    |
| Cuarenta  | y   | dos  | (42)  | Cuarenta y dos   |
| Cincuenta | y   | tres | (53)  | Cincuenta y tres |
| ...       | y   | ...  | ...   | ...              |
| Cien      |     |      | (100) | Cien             |

Explanation: Think of it like lego blocks, where you have your tens block and you are just adding another small block (the units) with "y" acting as the glue.

<TextToSpeechPlayer mp3File="/src/assets/courses/spanish/_shared/lessons/lesson4/audio/numbers-31-and-above.mp3" />

Note: For 21, 31, 41, etc., when "uno" is used after "veinti," "treinta," "cuarenta," etc., it becomes "un" or "una" depending on the noun it refers to, especially in counting objects or age.

**For example:**

Tengo veitiun libros (books are masculine).

Tengo veintiuna revistas (magazines are feminine).

Soy treinta y un años (age is masculine).

Ella tiene cuarenta y una amigas (friends are feminine).

-   Numbers like 22, 23, 24, etc., remain the same regardless of the noun's gender.

## Section 4: Using Numbers in Context

Numbers are used in many daily situations. Here are some phrases to practice:

-   ¿Cuántos años tienes? (How old are you?)
-   Tengo veintiún años. (I am twenty-one years old.)
-   ¿Cuánto cuesta esto? (How much does this cost?)
-   Son treinta euros. (It's thirty euros.)

# Lesson 3: Numbers and Counting

## Introduction

In this lesson, we’ll learn about numbers and how to count in German. Numbers are essential in everyday life as they appear in contexts like shopping, dates, telling time, and many more. Let's get started with the basics of counting in German.

## Section 4: Counting in Context

### Prices
- "Das kostet **einundzwanzig** Euro und **dreißig** Cent." (That costs twenty-one euros and thirty cents.)
- "Die Jacke ist **neunundvierzig** Euro **neunundneunzig**." (The jacket is forty-nine euros ninety-nine.)
- "Drei Brötchen kosten zusammen **zwei** Euro **fünfzig**." (Three rolls cost two euros fifty in total.)
- "Das Buch kostet **fünfzehn** Euro **zehn**." (The book costs fifteen euros ten.)

<TextToSpeechPlayer mp3File={/src/assets/audio/lessons/german/lesson3/prices.mp3} />

### Dates
- "Heute ist der **erste** Mai." (Today is the first of May.)
- "Mein Geburtstag ist am **dreiundzwanzigsten** April." (My birthday is on the twenty-third of April.)
- "Die Veranstaltung findet am **vierzehnten** Juni statt." (The event takes place on the fourteenth of June.)
- "Wir treffen uns am **siebten** Juli." (We are meeting on the seventh of July.)

<TextToSpeechPlayer mp3File={/src/assets/audio/lessons/german/lesson3/dates.mp3} />

### Time
- "Es ist drei Uhr **fünfzehn**." (It is three fifteen.)
- "Der Zug kommt um **vierzehn** Uhr zweiunddreißig an." (The train arrives at fourteen thirty-two.)
- "Der Film beginnt um **neunzehn** Uhr fünf." (The movie starts at nineteen oh five.)
- "Das Geschäft öffnet um **acht** Uhr." (The store opens at eight o'clock.)

<TextToSpeechPlayer mp3File={/src/assets/audio/lessons/german/lesson3/time.mp3} />

### Quantities
- "Ich hätte gerne **zwei** Kilo Äpfel." (I would like two kilos of apples.)
- "Bitte geben Sie mir **anderthalb** Liter Milch." (Please give me one and a half liters of milk.)
- "Ich brauche **drei Dutzend** Eier für das Rezept." (I need three dozen eggs for the recipe.)
- "Kann ich **vierhundert** Gramm Käse haben?" (Can I have four hundred grams of cheese?)

## Exercises

1. **Listen and Repeat:** Play the audio clips for each section and repeat the numbers to practice your pronunciation.
2. **Number Dictation:** Have someone say random numbers in German while you write them down.
3. **Find the Numbers:** Look for numbers in your environment (e.g., street numbers, prices) and say them in German.

## Conclusion

Congratulations on completing Lesson 3! Numbers form the basis for many everyday interactions, and being able to count confidently is a key skill in learning German. Continue practicing and soon you'll be able to use numbers effortlessly in your conversations.

# Lesson 2: Greetings and Introductions

## Introduction

In this lesson, we will learn how to greet people and introduce ourselves in German. Knowing these basic phrases is crucial for making a good first impression and is the foundation of starting conversations in German.

## Section 1: Basic Greetings

Here are some common German greetings that you will use throughout the day:

|     | German           | English       | Pronunciation Note                 |
| --- | ---------------- | ------------- | ---------------------------------- |
| 1   | Hallo!           | Hello!        |                                    |
| 2   | Guten Morgen!    | Good morning! | In the morning until about 10am    |
| 3   | Guten Tag!       | Good day!     | From mid-morning to late afternoon |
| 4   | Guten Abend!     | Good evening! | In the evening                     |
| 5   | Gute Nacht!      | Good night!   | Before going to bed                |
| 6   | Tschüss!         | Bye!          | Informal                           |
| 7   | Auf Wiedersehen! | Goodbye!      | Formal                             |

To introduce yourself, you can use the following phrases:

| 2   | Mein Name ist...                 | My name is...        |                                             |

| 6   | Freut mich, Sie kennenzulernen!  | Pleased to meet you! | Formal, when speaking directly to someone   |
| 7   | Das ist...                       | This is...           | When introducing someone else               |

## Section 3: Asking How Someone Is

After greeting, it's polite to ask how someone is doing:

|     | German                         | English              | Context Note                                  |
| --- | ------------------------------ | -------------------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| 1   | Wie geht's? (Wie geht es dir?) | How are you?         | Informal                                      |
| 2   | Wie geht es Ihnen?             | How are you?         | Formal                                        |
| 3   | Mir geht's gut, danke.         | I'm good, thank you. | In response to 'Wie geht's?'                  |
| 4   | Nicht schlecht, danke.         | Not bad, thank you.  | Another possible response                     |
| 5   | Und dir? / Und Ihnen?          | And you?             | To return the question, informally / formally |

## Exercises

1. **Listening and Repeating:** Play the audio clips and repeat each phrase to practice your pronunciation.
2. **Role-Playing:** Practice with a partner. One person greets and the other responds, then switch roles.
3. **Introduction Writing:** Write a short paragraph introducing yourself in German.

## Conclusion

With these basic greetings and introductions, you are now equipped to handle common social interactions in German-speaking environments.

Practice these phrases until you feel confident in your pronunciation and try to use them in real-life situations or with a language exchange partner to enhance your learning experience.
